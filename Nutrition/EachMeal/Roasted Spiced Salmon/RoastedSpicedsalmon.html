Roasted Spiced Salmon

A recipe that highlights the fish, you'll serve it with lemony spinach and rice pilaf that has juicy bursts of golden raisins and a sweet cinnamon aroma. It's elegant enough to serve in company and quick enough to be assembled on a weeknight.

Roasted Spiced Salmon

This recipe goes the extra mile to draw flavor from the fish with a compound butter made from turmeric and cumin which infuses each bite with spiced buttery flavor.

Preparation time: 30 mins

Total time: 40 mins

American cuisine

For 4 people

Ingredients:

  • ¼ cup olive oil, divided
  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • ½ cup golden raisins
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons kosher salt, divided
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • ½ teaspoon coarsely chopped cumin seeds
  • 4 6-ounce skin-on salmon fillets
  • 4 cups packed fresh baby spinach
  • 1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice (from 1 lemon)
  • ⅛ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

Preparation of the recipe:

  1. Preheat oven to 325 F.
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a medium saucepan over medium.
  3. Add rice, raisins, and cinnamon stick.
  4. Cook, stirring often, until rice is fragrant and slightly opaque, about 2 minutes.
  5. Stir in 2 cups water and 1½ teaspoons salt; bring to a boil over high.
  6. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer, undisturbed, until liquid is absorbed, about 10 minutes.
  7. Remove from heat and let stand, covered, until rice is just tender, about 10 minutes.
  8. Fluff with a fork.
  9. Meanwhile, mash butter, turmeric, cumin, and ½ teaspoon salt in a small bowl.
  10. Place salmon, skin side down, in a 13-by-9-inch baking dish.
  11. Spread butter mixture over salmon; cover with aluminum foil.
  12. Bake until salmon is firm when squeezed along sides, 20 to 25 minutes.
  13. Toss spinach, lemon juice, pepper, and remaining 2 tablespoons oil and ¼ teaspoon salt in a medium bowl.
  14. Serve salad alongside salmon and rice.
